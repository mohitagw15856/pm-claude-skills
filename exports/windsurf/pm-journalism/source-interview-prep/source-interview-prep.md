# Source Interview Prep Skill

A journalistic interview isn't a friendly chat or a research call — it's an on-the-record exchange where the subject may have every reason not to answer straight. This skill preps the interview to get truthful, usable, attributable answers: rapport first, the hard questions sequenced so they don't end the conversation early, and a plan for the dodge.

## Working from a brief

Given the subject and the story, **write the full prep** — infer where the subject is cooperative vs. defensive and sequence accordingly. Always handle ground rules explicitly; never counsel deception or entrapment.

## Required Inputs

Ask for (if not provided, else infer and label):
- **Who** you're interviewing and their **relationship to the story** (witness, expert, accountable party, whistleblower)
- **What you need** — the facts, quotes, or confirmation this interview must produce
- **The dynamic** — cooperative, reluctant, or adversarial; on/off record expectations

## Output Format

### Ground rules (settle first)
On-the-record vs. background vs. off-the-record vs. not-for-attribution — define them, agree them up front, and note how you'll handle mid-interview attempts to go off-record.

### Question plan (sequenced)
1. **Open / rapport** — easy, establishing questions.
2. **Build the record** — the factual, chronological questions.
3. **The core asks** — the questions the story needs, specific and answerable.
4. **The hard questions** — accountability/contradiction questions, placed after you have the record (so they can't just walk).
5. **Close** — "what did I not ask?" and "who else should I talk to?"

### Handling the dodge
Techniques for the evasive or hostile subject: the follow-up that closes the gap, repeating the unanswered question, using documents/quotes to press, silence, and staying neutral rather than arguing.

### Capture plan
Recording (and consent/legal note — recording-consent laws vary by jurisdiction), notes, and how you'll verify quotes.

## Quality Checks

- [ ] Ground rules (on/off record, attribution) are defined and agreed before the hard part
- [ ] Questions are sequenced rapport → record → core → hard, not front-loaded with accusations
- [ ] The core asks are specific and answerable, tied to what the story needs
- [ ] There's a concrete plan for evasive/hostile responses
- [ ] Capture includes a recording-consent/legal note (laws vary by jurisdiction)
- [ ] No advice to deceive, entrap, or misrepresent who you are

## Anti-Patterns

- Leading with the accusatory question (the subject shuts down or leaves)
- Never settling on/off-record, then fighting about it later
- Vague questions that invite a non-answer
- Arguing with or editorializing at the subject instead of pressing on facts
- No plan for the dodge — accepting the first evasion
- Recording without regard for consent law
