# Worked Example — The Thesis Defense

A gold-standard run of this skill: Lena's gig-economy thesis (from the thesis-outline worked example) defended before the real committee meets — with her dread question arriving reworded, one bluff punished, and one honest "I don't know" rewarded.

## The brief

Lena's sociology MA thesis argues that food-delivery couriers exercise agency through *algorithmic mastery* — optimization within platform rules — a third term the compliance/resistance binary misses. Evidence: 22 interviews (one city, one platform) + 6 months of meetup ethnography. Her dread question: "isn't 'mastery' just resistance you've renamed?" Committee: her supervisor (interview-centric, resistance-skeptic), a quantitative methodologist, and an adjacent-field labor economist. Format: 20-minute presentation + 60 minutes of questions, pass-with-revisions culture.

## The output

# Defense: "Working the algorithm" — MA, closed defense

> Simulation — a plausible adversarial reading, not a prediction.

## Transcript (excerpted)

**The Methodologist:** "Twenty-two interviews, one city, one platform. Walk me through what the word 'couriers' in your title is entitled to mean."
**Lena:** "It means the couriers I studied — the thesis claims the *existence* of mastery practices, not their prevalence. Chapter 3 pre-commits to that: practice-existence claims, never rates."
*Follow-up:* "Then defend the title." — "Fair. If the committee prefers, 'couriers on one platform' is a title revision I'd accept." *(The simulation notes: conceding a small, true point early buys credibility for the load-bearing ones.)*

**The Alternative-Explainer (economist):** "Couldn't your 'mastery' simply be selection? The couriers who attend meetups — where you recruited — are the optimizers. You've sampled enthusiasm and called it a category."
**Lena (bluffing):** "The ethnography controls for that." 
*Follow-up, immediate:* "How? Describe the control." — the bluff has nowhere to go; six months of field notes don't *control* for recruitment selection, they deepen it.
*The recovery the debrief supplies:* "It can't rule selection out — recruitment through meetups oversamples engaged couriers, and chapter 3 should say so. What it *can* claim: the practices exist, are shared socially, and are articulated as pride rather than resistance — which is the claim the thesis needs."

**The Scope-Enforcer/Supervisor:** "Chapter 6 says mastery 'reshapes how we should theorize algorithmic control.' One city. One platform. Reshape?"
**Lena:** "I don't know how far it generalizes — honestly. What I can defend: the binary fails *at least here*, and one well-documented counterexample obligates the framework to account for it. I'd accept softening 'reshapes' to 'challenges.'"
*(The simulation rewards this — the honest boundary plus the retreat-to-defensible-ground is exactly what committees want to watch a candidate do.)*

**The dread question, reworded (Generous-Elder tone):** "Help me see what 'mastery' buys us that 'everyday resistance' — which the literature already has — doesn't."
**Lena (prepared):** "Everyday resistance opposes the system's goals covertly. My couriers *adopt* the system's goals and beat it at its own scoring — they describe pride, not subversion, and their practices raise platform metrics rather than degrade them. A category that predicts opposite affect and opposite metric effects isn't a renaming — it's a different animal, and chapter 5's re-reading of the interviews against the resistance frameworks is the evidence."

## The Deliberation
Pass with revisions — likely regardless; the *quality* of revisions demanded depends on the defense. This run: title-scope softening, a selection-effects limitation section, and the "challenges" edit — all pre-conceded or prepared, which is the deliberation's private definition of a good defense. The real assessment vs. the room's polite version: the methodologist will call the sampling answer "adequate"; privately it was the run's weakest sustained ground.

## Debrief — out of character

| Weakness exposed | Preparable? | The answer-shape that holds |
|---|---|---|
| Meetup-recruitment selection | Yes — write the limitation *into* ch. 3 before the defense | Concede the mechanism, retreat to existence-claims, keep the affect/metrics evidence |
| Title overreach | Yes — trivially | Offer the revision before they demand it |
| "Reshapes" in ch. 6 | Yes | The one-counterexample-obligates move + "challenges" |
| The ethnography-as-control bluff | **The lesson** | Never claim methodology does what it doesn't; the honest version was *stronger* |

**The opening-summary rewrite:** lead with the affect/metrics distinction (pride + improved metrics ≠ resistance) — it's the thesis's best fact and it pre-answers the dread question.
**Volunteer these limitations:** recruitment selection, single-platform scope — volunteered reads as maturity; extracted reads as concealment.

## Why it's shaped this way

- **Every question traces to the supplied thesis** — the selection probe comes from her stated recruitment method; the scope probe from her own chapter 6 verb — per the rule that absences and overreaches in the material ARE the exam.
- **The bluff was punished immediately and the honest "I don't know" rewarded** — the framework's core training pair, both staged on purpose so the debrief can contrast their trajectories.
- **The dread question arrived reworded and gentler** — dread questions usually do; preparing the *content* rather than the phrasing is the lesson.
- **The deliberation separates the polite room from the real assessment** — "adequate" vs. weakest-ground, per the quality checks.
- **Every debrief row ends in a rehearsable answer-shape** — concede-retreat-hold structures, not "be more confident" — because shapes rehearse and vibes don't.
