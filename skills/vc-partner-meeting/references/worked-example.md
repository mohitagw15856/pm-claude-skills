# Worked Example — VC Partner Meeting

## The brief

Nalu, a seed-stage startup, pitched Thursday: workflow software for physical-therapy clinics, $45k MRR growing 11% m/m, 62 clinics, founder was a PT for 9 years + a technical co-founder, raising $3M at $15M post. Known objection: "isn't this a feature of the EHR?" Fund context: $300M multi-stage.

## The output

# Partner Meeting: Nalu — Monday, 9:40

> Simulation — a plausible adversarial reading, not a prediction or investment advice.

## Transcript

**CHAMPION:** The founder spent nine years inside the problem. Sixty-two clinics with essentially no sales team, 11% monthly — that's pull. PT clinics are drowning in documentation and the EHRs there are ancient.

**PATTERN-MATCHER:** I've seen this movie — vertical SaaS on top of clinical workflows. It's either "wedge into billing and become the system of record," which is Weave or Jane, or it's a documentation tool the EHR absorbs in two release cycles.

**SKEPTIC:** The deck never says who *pays*. Clinic owner? The PT? At $189/clinic/month, $45k MRR checks out — but the objection isn't "is it useful," it's §competition: WebPT has 40% of outpatient clinics. What happens when they ship a worse version for free?

**CHAMPION:** The same thing that always happens — the incumbent's version is bad for five years, and by then the wedge is the workflow layer clinicians actually open.

**FUND-MATH:** Let me do the boring part. $3M at $15M post gets us 20% if we take it all. For this to return a third of the fund, that 20% needs to be worth $300M — a $1.5B outcome, minus dilution call it $2.5B. Vertical SaaS in a market with maybe 40k US clinics… $189/mo × 40k clinics is $90M TAM on current pricing. The math needs payments or staffing revenue, and the deck only gestures at that.

**SKEPTIC:** Notice the deck has no churn number. Growth without churn is a numerator without a denominator.

**PATTERN-MATCHER:** To be fair — founder-market fit here is the real kind. Nine years of clinical practice reads.

**FUND-MATH:** I'd want it at Series A with the payments motion live. Right now it's a good company, possibly not a fund-returner.

**CHAMPION:** That's how we miss the vertical winners — everyone's TAM math on Toast was wrong the same way.

## Internal Memo

**Verdict:** TRACK
**Anti-thesis:** strong founder-market fit and organic pull, but current pricing yields sub-$100M TAM; fund-returner case requires an unproven payments/staffing expansion.
**What we'd need to believe:** (1) churn < 1.5%/mo — number currently absent; (2) payments attach ≥ 20% in a pilot; (3) WebPT's distribution doesn't convert to product inside 18 months.
**Deal terms discussed:** at $15M post we'd want 20%; more likely: lead their A at $2M+ MRR with expansion revenue visible.
**TRACK milestone:** come back at $150k MRR with churn + payments-pilot data. The associate's email will say "keep us posted on your progress" — the real reason is TAM math, stated above.

## Debrief — out of character

| Objection raised | Fixable before the real meeting? | How |
|---|---|---|
| No churn number | ✅ Tonight | Put gross + logo churn on the traction slide; if it's good it's your best slide, if it's bad they'll find it anyway |
| TAM at current pricing | ✅ Partially | Reprice the story: clinics → payments/staffing attach with the pilot LOI you mentioned verbally; verbal ≠ slide |
| "EHR will absorb it" | ⚠️ Reframe only | Daily-active workflow vs system-of-record argument, with WAU/clinic data |
| Fund-size mismatch | ❌ Structural | A $300M fund needs the $2.5B story; a $75M seed fund doesn't — target list matters as much as the deck |

Strongest single move: **add the churn slide and the payments pilot with a named clinic count** — it converts the Fund-Math partner's pass into the Skeptic's problem, and the Skeptic was half-won already.

## Why it's shaped this way

- **The partners disagree and stay disagreed** — the memo verdict follows fund math over enthusiasm, which is how real meetings resolve.
- **Fund math is computed in-transcript** ($3M/20% → $2.5B needed) — the skill demands arithmetic, not "TAM concerns."
- **The missing churn number becomes dialogue** — partners noticing silences is the realistic tell the founder needs.
- **TRACK carries a named milestone and the polite-email vs real-reason split** — the skill's honesty rule about pass language.
- **The debrief separates tonight-fixable from structural** — including the uncomfortable "wrong fund size" finding no deck edit repairs.
