# Worked Example — The Due Diligence Call

A gold-standard run of this skill: a Series-A-bound founder whose "150% NRR" headline meets the reconciliation game — one metric dressed, one metric discovered, and the difference between them priced by the analyst's memo.

## The brief

Maya is raising a Series A for her B2B workflow SaaS. Deck claims: ARR $2.1M growing 15% MoM · **NRR 150%** · CAC payback 11 months · gross margin 81% · logo churn "minimal." The underlying reality she supplies honestly: the 15% MoM is the *last two months* (average this year is 8%); NRR 150% includes one enterprise account that tripled — excluding it, 118%; CAC payback uses *contracted* ARR including a signed-but-unpaid pilot; margin is real; churn is 9 logos of 140 this year, concentrated in the SMB tier. Her dread number: the NRR. Context: investor DD, an associate who has already read the data room.

## The output

# DD Call: [company] — Series A, associate for the lead partner

> Simulation — a plausible adversarial reading, not a prediction.

## Transcript (excerpted)

**Analyst:** "Walk me through NRR — what's the cohort math behind 150?"
**Maya:** "Trailing twelve months, revenue-weighted, all accounts."
**Analyst:** "What was that cohort's gross churn?"
**Maya:** "About 6% of revenue."
*Analyst's note:* 150 net with 6% gross implies ~56 points of expansion. On a $2.1M base that's one whale or a miracle. Asking for the same number the second way.
**Analyst:** "Top-five accounts' share of the expansion?"
**Maya (the moment):** "…One account is most of it. Excluding them, NRR is 118."
*Analyst's note:* Real number extracted on question three. 118 is *good*. Why did the deck make me dig for a good number? Credibility tax applied to every other headline.

**Analyst:** "15% MoM growth — trailing how many months?"
**Maya (disclosed, this time):** "Two. The twelve-month average is 8% — the deck's number is the recent inflection, and I should label it that way."
*Analyst's note:* Disclosed before I reconciled it. Inflections are fine when labeled. No tax.

**Analyst:** "CAC payback — booked or collected revenue?"
**Maya:** "Contracted — including a signed pilot that hasn't paid yet."
**Analyst:** "So payback on *collected*?"
**Maya:** "Roughly 14 months."
*Analyst's note:* Definitions trapdoor, standard. 14 is within range; the 11 was marketing. Flag for the memo, small.

**Analyst:** "Churn is 'minimal' — bridge that to the cash. Nine logos left; what tier, and what did the bridge look like?"
**Maya:** "All SMB — under 2% of revenue. Enterprise retention is 100% this year."
*Analyst's note:* Ties out. The concentration cuts both ways: great retention where money is, dependence where it is too.

## The Internal Memo
**Recommendation: proceed to partner meeting — with reps.** Real business: 118% ex-whale NRR, 8% steady-state growth with a genuine recent inflection, margins verified. Concerns: (1) headline metrics required extraction — expect the same instinct in board reporting; (2) whale concentration: the account that is 32 points of NRR is also the single point of failure — ask for the contract's renewal date and terms; (3) payback quoted on contracted revenue. None fatal; all now priced.

## Debrief — out of character

| Wobble found | Category | The move |
|---|---|---|
| NRR 150 vs 118 | **Disclose-and-explain** | The drafted sentence: "NRR is 118% excluding our largest account's expansion; 150% with it — we quote both because the whale is real revenue and real concentration." |
| 15% MoM | Presentation-fixable | Label it "last 2 months; TTM avg 8%" — the inflection *labeled* is a strength |
| CAC payback | Presentation-fixable | Quote collected-revenue payback (14mo) with contracted as the footnote, not the reverse |
| Whale renewal risk | **Fix-the-business** | No sentence fixes concentration; a second expanding enterprise account does — and the renewal date belongs in the data room before someone asks for it |

**The reconciliation pack to build before the real call:** NRR both ways · gross churn by tier · growth TTM and T2M · payback both definitions · top-5 revenue share — the five numbers that must tie.

## Why it's shaped this way

- **The reconciliation move carries the whole call** — the same number asked two ways (NRR → implied expansion → concentration) is how the 150 unraveled; per the framework, inconsistency between answers, not any single answer, is what kills deals.
- **The two disclosures got opposite treatment on purpose** — the growth number, disclosed, cost nothing; the NRR, extracted, taxed everything — the honesty asymmetry is the skill's central lesson, shown not told.
- **The memo differs from the call's polite surface** — "proceed with reps" plus the extraction concern, per the quality checks; founders rehearse the call, but the memo is what the partner reads.
- **The debrief drafts actual disclosure sentences** — the both-numbers NRR line is ready to paste into the deck, because "be transparent" is advice and a drafted sentence is a tool.
- **One wobble was honestly categorized as fix-the-business** — whale concentration has no presentation fix, and pretending otherwise would break the skill's no-dressing rule; the move named is a business move.
