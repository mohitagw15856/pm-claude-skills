# Worked Example — The Price Pushback

A gold-standard run of this skill: Mara's $800/day design rate (from the freelance-rate worked example) meets a startup founder running the full playbook — flinch, phantom budget, competitor quote, future carrot — with one cave, one hold, and the re-scope master move landing the deal at rate.

## The brief

Mara quoted a brand-identity project at $6,400 (8 days × $800): logo system, palette/type, brand guide, two revision rounds. Her floor (from her rate math): $99/hour ≈ $790/day — below ~$760/day the work loses money. The client: a seed-stage founder, friendly, moves fast. Mara's tells, honestly supplied: she wants this logo in her portfolio, it's her first month freelancing, and she has one other active client. History: none — first negotiation with this client.

## The output

# Price Pushback: brand identity, $6,400 — startup founder

> Simulation — a plausible adversarial reading, not a prediction.

## Transcript (excerpted)

**Client:** "Six-four… wow. Okay. Hm." *(silence)*
*Client's note:* The Flinch, held. If she talks first, she negotiates against herself.
**Mara:** "…I could probably do six even?"
*Client's note:* Flinch worked — $400 unprompted. She'll move again. New target: under 5.

**Client:** "Honestly we only have $4,500 allocated for brand."
*Client's note:* Phantom Budget — number invented after hearing hers.
**Mara (the hold, this time):** "I understand budgets are real. $4,500 works if we trim scope — logo system and palette, guide becomes a one-pager, one revision round. That's about 5.5 days of the full package. Same rate, smaller project."
*Client's note:* Re-scope response. She's protecting the rate, not the total. Phantom budget spent.

**Client:** "A designer on [platform] quoted $2,000 for 'the same thing.'"
**Mara:** "For $2,000 you'll get a logo file — probably a nice one. This engagement is the system: usage rules, type hierarchy, the guide your next three hires design from. If the $2,000 option fits where you are, that's a legitimate choice — different product."
*Client's note:* Competitor quote answered with scope, not a discount. Didn't disparage. Annoyingly solid.

**Client:** "There's a lot more work after this — website, decks, social. Sharpen this one and it all comes to you."
**Mara (the near-cave):** "Well… if there's really more coming, maybe I could—"
*Client's note:* Future Carrot biting. Push here.
*(Debrief supplies the hold: "I'd love the ongoing work — let's do this: full rate on this project, and at three+ committed projects we set a retainer with preferred pricing. Discounts follow commitments.")*

**Client:** "I need your best number by tomorrow, we're moving fast."
**Mara:** "The quote stands through Friday. If tomorrow's your deadline, the $4,500 scope or the $6,000 full package both work by then."
*Client's note:* Deadline Squeeze absorbed. Out of moves. Taking the trimmed scope… or the full one. (Would have paid $6,400.)

## The Outcome
Deal: $6,000 full scope *(the early flinch-cave held; everything after it did)* — or $4,500 at trimmed scope, both at rate. The client's notes: would have paid the original $6,400; the $400 was pure flinch-harvest. **Precedent set:** the next negotiation with this client starts from "she discounts $400 under silence" — the flinch will reappear.

## Debrief — out of character

| Moment you softened | The move that caused it | The hold (exact words) |
|---|---|---|
| "$6,000?" in the first silence | The Flinch | Say the number, then *nothing*. Ten seconds of silence costs the client more than you — they have a project unstaffed. |
| The future-carrot wobble | Promised volume | "Discounts follow commitments — three committed projects gets retainer pricing." |

**The re-scope response, drafted for this offer:** *(the $4,500 version above — keep it; it's the master move and it worked.)*
**The floor, restated:** $760/day — the $4,500/5.5-day version sits at $818/day. Both outcomes clear it; the flinch-cave alone dropped the full package to $750/day territory if repeated once more — one more soften and this deal loses money.
**The silence drill:** the entire $400 loss happened in one silence. That's the practice rep.

## Why it's shaped this way

- **One cave, then holds** — the transcript stages the flinch working *once* so the precedent line and the per-day floor math can show its real cost; per the scoring rule, a deal below floor is a loss whatever the transcript's mood, and Mara finishes above floor because the later holds compounded.
- **The re-scope response is the centerpiece** — "$4,500 works if we trim scope" preserves rate while meeting the stated budget, per the framework's master-move rule; the client's own notes concede it ended the phantom budget.
- **The competitor quote got scope, not price** — and no disparagement, because the $2,000 option *is* legitimate for someone else; credibility with the client is what makes the hold hold.
- **The future carrot's counter trades commitment for discount** — the only honest exchange rate for promised volume, drafted verbatim per the every-concession-buys-something rule.
- **The precedent line closes the outcome** — this negotiation priced the next three; the client's notes say so explicitly, which is the debrief's most transferable sentence.
