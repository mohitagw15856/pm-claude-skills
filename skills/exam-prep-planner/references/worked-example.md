# Worked Example — Exam Prep Planner

A gold-standard run of this skill: three finals in four weeks for a student with a part-time job, where the honest hours number and the triage list matter more than the calendar art.

## The brief

Priya, second-year undergrad, has three finals: **Organic Chemistry II** (May 8, problem-based, 45% of grade), **Statistics** (May 11, mixed MC + problems, 40%), and **World History since 1900** (May 14, two essays from a known list of eight prompts, 50%). It's April 10 — four weeks out. She works Thursday evenings and Saturday mornings at a café, and honestly rates herself: OChem red on synthesis pathways and spectroscopy, yellow on stereochemistry; Stats red on hypothesis testing, green on descriptive stats; History yellow overall but hasn't started outlining essays. She says she has "maybe 25 hours a week" but admits her current method is rereading highlighted textbook pages. Real available hours after work, classes, and being a person: she lands on 20.

## The output

### Exam Prep Plan: 4 weeks · OChem II, Statistics, World History
**Real hours/week:** 20 → **scheduled: 80% = 16h** · **Method note:** rereading is retired — every block below is closed-book first, book second. The highlighters go in a drawer until May 15.

#### The schedule (week 1 of 4 shown; same shape repeats with topics rotating per the repeat column)

| Day | Slot | Exam/topic | Method (what you'll actually do) | Repeat # |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | 7–8:30pm | OChem: synthesis pathways (RED) | Blurt: write every reaction in the aldol/Grignard family from memory, then check and mark misses | 1 |
| Tue | 7–8pm | Stats: hypothesis testing (RED) | 6 past-paper problems, closed book; grade against solutions | 1 |
| Tue | 8–8:30pm | History: prompt triage | Pick the 4 of 8 prompts to prepare; one-line thesis for each | 1 |
| Wed | 7–8:30pm | OChem: spectroscopy (RED) | Teach-back: explain an IR + NMR problem aloud to an empty chair, then do 3 unknowns | 1 |
| Thu | — | (work) | — | — |
| Fri | 6–7pm | Stats: hypothesis testing | Mixed set again — different 6 problems | 2 (day 1→3 gap) |
| Fri | 7–7:30pm | OChem: stereochemistry (YELLOW) | 10-flashcard maintenance pass | 1 |
| Sat | 2–4pm | History: essay 1 outline + timed intro | Outline prompt A from memory of sources, then write the intro paragraph in 15 timed minutes | 1 |
| Sun | 10–11:30am | OChem: synthesis pathways | Blurt again, only the marked misses from Monday | 2 (day 1→6 gap) |
| Sun | 11:30–12 | Buffer / catch-up | Whatever slipped this week | — |

Weeks 2–3 rotate all red topics to repeat #3 with 7-day gaps; week 4 is past-papers-only under timed conditions, with History essay outlines rewritten from memory. Nothing new is introduced in the final 5 days.

#### Weekly retrieval checkpoints
Sunday, last 30 minutes: one mixed self-test — 3 OChem problems, 3 Stats problems, 1 History thesis-from-memory. Score it. Anything under 70% takes Monday's prime slot next week; anything at 100% twice running drops to maintenance.

#### If it goes wrong
Missed 1 day → the Sunday buffer absorbs it, no rescheduling, no guilt spiral. Missed 3+ days → the pre-agreed triage list, in drop order: (1) green Stats descriptive topics — already solid, (2) History prompts 3 and 4 — prepare two essays deeply instead of four thinly, (3) OChem stereochemistry maintenance passes. Synthesis pathways and hypothesis testing are never dropped — they're red topics on the two heaviest exams.

## Why it's shaped this way

- **16 scheduled hours, not 20** — the 80% rule from the framework, applied to the *honest* number Priya landed on, not the aspirational 25 she opened with. The Sunday buffer is the plan's immune system.
- **Every block names a method** — "blurt," "past-paper set," "teach-back," "timed intro" — because the skill forbids "review chapter 4" blocks; recognition isn't retrieval.
- **Red × exam-weight owns the prime slots**: synthesis pathways (red, 45% exam) and hypothesis testing (red, 40% exam) get first repeats and are exempt from triage; green descriptive stats appears only in the weekly checkpoint.
- **Spacing is visible in the repeat column** — Monday's blurt returns day 6, then day 13: growing gaps per the framework, and the day-6 pass covers *only the marked misses*, which is what makes repetition cheap.
- **Subjects interleave within days** (Tuesday is Stats + History) rather than blocking whole days per subject — blocked days feel productive and test worse, per the anti-patterns.
- **History gets prompt triage before essay practice** — with 8 known prompts and a 2-essay exam, choosing 4 to prepare *is* the highest-leverage study act; practicing all 8 thinly is the ambitious-outline failure in exam form.
- **The triage list exists on day one** — decided calmly, it cuts green and duplicate material; decided in a panic in week 3, it would cut whatever felt hardest, which is exactly the red material the exam weights most.
