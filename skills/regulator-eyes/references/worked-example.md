# Worked Example — Regulator Eyes

## The brief

A sleep-supplement DTC brand ("Somnora") pastes its new landing page before a paid-traffic push. Copy includes: "Clinically proven to double your deep sleep," "94% of users fall asleep faster," a doctor in a white coat, before/after sleep-tracker screenshots, "#1 Sleep Formula," and a countdown timer: "Sale ends in 04:59." Evidence on hand: one 40-person study run by the ingredient supplier, and a customer survey (n=212, 61% response).

## The output

# Regulatory Reading: Somnora landing page — pre-launch

> Simulation — a plausible adversarial reading, not a prediction or legal advice.

## Claim Inventory

| # | Claim (verbatim) | Type | Substantiation required | User has it? | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Clinically proven to double your deep sleep" | Express, health | Competent & reliable scientific evidence: typically ≥1 well-controlled human trial *of the finished product*, replicating "double" | ❌ Supplier study: n=40, ingredient-only, 22% improvement — supports neither "proven" nor "double" | 🔴 |
| 2 | "94% of users fall asleep faster" | Express, survey-based | Survey methodology adequate: representative sample, neutral wording | ⚠️ n=212 self-selected respondents; 94% is of *responders* | 🔴 |
| 3 | Doctor imagery + "Formulated with physicians" | Implied endorsement | Actual physician involvement; disclosure of any payment | ⚠️ One paid advisor — undisclosed | 🟡 |
| 4 | Before/after tracker screenshots | Implied typicality | Results must be typical or clearly disclaimed *with* the typical result stated | ❌ Cherry-picked, no typicality data | 🔴 |
| 5 | "#1 Sleep Formula" | Express superiority (or puffery?) | If ranked: the ranking's source. Unranked "#1" in a category with measurable claims reads as factual | 🟡 |
| 6 | Countdown "Sale ends in 04:59" | Format/dark pattern | Timer must reflect a real ending sale | ❌ Timer resets per visit | 🟡 |
| 7 | "Wake up refreshed" | Puffery | None — subjective feel-statement | — | 🟢 |

## The Inquiry Letter

> **CIVIL INVESTIGATIVE DEMAND — In re: Somnora Labs, advertising of "Somnora Deep Sleep"**
>
> Pursuant to its authority under Section 5, staff requests within 30 days: (1) all substantiation for the claim "clinically proven to double your deep sleep," including complete study protocols, raw data, and the identity and funding of the study sponsor; (2) the methodology, instrument, response rate, and complete results of any consumer survey supporting "94% of users fall asleep faster"; (3) documents sufficient to identify all compensation to physicians appearing in or consulted for the advertising; (4) all data supporting the typicality of results depicted in before/after imagery; (5) documents describing the operation of countdown timers displayed on somnora.com.

## Debrief — out of character

| # | Verdict | New wording / required disclosure |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Drop** "proven to double." Reword: "Contains [ingredient], shown in a preliminary 40-person study to improve deep sleep by 22%." | Fund a finished-product study before any "proven" language returns |
| 2 | **Reword + disclose:** "94% of surveyed customers who responded (212 of 350) reported…" — or drop to the survey's honest headline | |
| 3 | **Disclose:** "Dr. — is a paid advisor to Somnora" adjacent to the image | |
| 4 | **Drop or fix:** typicality disclosure only works with the typical number — "typical users saw 15–25 min improvement" + keep the screenshot | |
| 5 | **Anchor or soften:** "#1" → cite the ranking, or move to clear puffery ("our best-loved formula") | |
| 6 | **Fix the timer** to a real end time or remove — resetting timers are the easiest screenshot a competitor files | |

*Confirm anything load-bearing with an advertising-law attorney — standards vary by jurisdiction and vertical; supplements sit in the high-scrutiny tier.*

## Why it's shaped this way

- **Implied claims get inventory rows** (the white coat, the screenshots) — the net-impression standard is the whole game, and literal-text-only audits miss it.
- **Each 🔴 names the specific missing substantiation** — "n=40, ingredient-only, 22%" against "proven to double" is the finding; "needs evidence" would be noise.
- **"Wake up refreshed" is cleared as puffery** — inflating everything to red destroys the signal, per the anti-patterns.
- **The letter demands the user's actual claims verbatim** — reading your own copy inside a CID format is the intended cold shower.
- **Every verdict ships replacement wording** — the debrief's job is a publishable page, not a burned one; note the 22% study survives *honestly stated*.
