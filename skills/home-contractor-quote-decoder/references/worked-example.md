# Worked Example — Home Contractor Quote Decoder

A gold-standard run of this skill: a $48,000 kitchen quote whose real expected cost is ~$61,000 — found via the allowance audit and the silent-items list before the contract was signed, instead of via change orders after.

## The brief

Tomas and Erin got a $48,000 quote for a full kitchen remodel ("demo, cabinets, counters, flooring, electrical & plumbing as required, paint"). The one-and-a-half-page document includes: cabinet allowance $6,000 · countertop allowance $2,800 · flooring allowance $1,800 · "electrical as required" · payment: 40% deposit, 30% at cabinet install, 30% at completion · exclusions: "permits by owner; disposal not included; appliances by owner" · timeline: "approx. 6–8 weeks" · no change-order clause. They've picked semi-custom cabinets and quartz counters at a showroom. A competing bid came in at $56,000 "all-in" and they're leaning toward the "cheaper" one.

## The output

### Contractor Quote Decode: kitchen remodel — [contractor], $48,000

**1. The verdict** — The realistic expected cost of this quote is **~$58,000–61,000**: the three allowances are $6,000–8,000 under your showroom selections, and the exclusions list quietly hands you $3,000–5,000 of disposal, permits, and patching. The $56,000 "all-in" bid may genuinely be the cheaper one — they can't be compared until this one's gaps are priced. Resolve the allowances and the payment schedule before signing anything.

**2. Section decode**

| Section | What it says | What it means / what's missing | Severity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scope | "demo, cabinets, counters, flooring, electrical & plumbing **as required**" | Outcome language — "as required" is decided later, by them, priced then | 🔴 |
| Cabinet allowance | $6,000 | Your semi-custom selection runs $10–12k for this layout — a placeholder wearing a price | 🔴 |
| Counters | $2,800 | Quartz for ~30 sq ft installed ≈ $4,500–6,000 | 🔴 |
| Flooring | $1,800 | Plausible-to-light depending on selection — confirm at your grade | 🟡 |
| Payment | 40/30/30 | 40% deposit is heavy, and 70% is paid by cabinet install — money well ahead of completed work (deposit caps vary — verify locally) | 🔴 |
| Change orders | *(absent)* | The mechanism you'll use most isn't in the document | 🔴 |
| Timeline | "approx. 6–8 wks" | No start window, no milestone dates | 🟡 |

**3. The allowance audit** — Cabinets +$4,000–6,000 · counters +$1,700–3,200 · flooring +$0–800 at their grade → **allowance overage subtotal: ~$5,700–10,000** against real showroom selections.

**4. The silent-items list** — permits (owner — cost *and* liability shifted; ask why), disposal (a kitchen demo is a dumpster + fees), drywall patching and paint touch-ups beyond the kitchen's walls, appliance hookup (excluded appliances usually means excluded *installation* — ask), under-cabinet lighting if "electrical as required" doesn't name it. Each is a future change order; priced now: **~$3,000–5,000**.

**5. Payment schedule read** — At cabinet install (roughly week 3–4 of 8), they hold 70% while perhaps 45% of the work exists. Restructure to request: 15–20% deposit (verify local caps), then milestone-tied payments (demo/rough-in pass, cabinet install, counters+flooring, completion with punch-list holdback of 10%).

**6. Making bids comparable** — Send both bidders: the enumerated scope (including disposal, permits, patching, hookup), your actual selections in place of allowances, a change-order clause, and the milestone schedule — then compare numbers that finally mean the same thing.

*"This is a plain-language reading, not legal/financial advice — laws vary by jurisdiction; confirm anything load-bearing with a qualified professional."*

## Why it's shaped this way

- **The finished-job reconciliation drove everything** — list what a *done* kitchen requires, find each item in the document as priced/allowanced/excluded/silent — that's the framework's core move, and the silent-items list is its output.
- **The allowance audit used their actual showroom selections** — $6,000 against a $10–12k choice is the teaser-allowance pattern; audited at *their* grade, per the framework, not at market abstractions.
- **The verdict re-priced the quote before comparing bids** — the $48k-vs-$56k comparison was meaningless until the gaps were counted; per the anti-patterns, the lowest bid is neither best nor worst until scope is leveled.
- **The payment read is money-vs-completed-work over time** — 70% at week 3–4 is the exposure, stated as a timeline, with the restructure to request and the verify-locally flag on deposit caps.
- **The absent change-order clause is a 🔴 finding, not a footnote** — a renovation's most-used mechanism missing from the document is the document's loudest sentence.
- **The contractor's honesty was never judged** — the decode reads the paper, flags the gaps, and routes reference-checking and licensing to separate homework, per the anti-patterns; bad documents come from good contractors all the time.
