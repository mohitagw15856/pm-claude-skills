# 🎓 For Educators — teach professional judgment with the library

Everything here is free for classroom use (MIT). The [6-week syllabus](../syllabus/) is the full course; this kit is the drop-in version: three ready-to-grade assignments, rubrics aligned to the library's own 0–40 scales, and the LMS path.

## Why professors use this
Students must *direct* AI, not dodge it. Every assignment below is AI-native by design: the deliverable includes the transcript, and the grade rewards briefing, reviewing, and rejecting — the skills employment actually tests now.

## The three assignments

### 1. The Brief-and-Review cycle (any course, 90 min + homework)
Students pick a skill matching your field (e.g. `incident-postmortem` for CS, `competitive-analysis` for business), run it on a provided messy brief, then **grade the AI's output** against the skill's own Scoring Rubric (0–40), documenting three specific failures and re-briefing to fix them. **Deliverable:** final artifact + both transcripts + their scored rubric. **You grade:** the quality of their *critique*, not the AI's prose.

### 2. The Negotiation Gym practical (management/comms courses)
Students play the [Gym](https://mohitagw15856.github.io/pm-claude-skills/gym.html) scenario twice minimum; submit their best attested transcript (SHA-256 built in) + a one-page reflection mapping their moves to the debrief dimensions. **You grade:** the reflection against the /40 debrief. Class leaderboard optional via a [Season](https://mohitagw15856.github.io/pm-claude-skills/casting.html)-style shared scenario.

### 3. The Anti-Pattern Hunt (writing-intensive courses)
Give students a deliberately flawed document (or generate one by prompting *without* a skill). They audit it against the matching skill's Anti-Patterns section, citing rule-by-rule from the [Museum](https://mohitagw15856.github.io/pm-claude-skills/museum.html). **Deliverable:** the annotated autopsy + the repaired version. **You grade:** rule citations and the repair.

## Grading rubrics
Each production skill ships a 0–40 rubric with 0/5/10 anchors (see any [skill page](https://mohitagw15856.github.io/pm-claude-skills/catalog.html)). Use them verbatim or import the [SCORM package](../syllabus/) into Canvas/Moodle/Blackboard.

## Academic integrity, stated plainly
The library's own skills enforce the line: `statement-coach` refuses to ghostwrite, `literature-review-builder` never invents citations and keeps an unread register. Our recommended policy language: *"AI use is permitted and expected; submissions include full transcripts; you are graded on your direction and judgment, which the transcript makes visible."*
